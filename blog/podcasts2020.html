Podcast Listening List

January 2020

I listen to a lot of podcasts. I am constantly unsubscribing and finding new podcasts I love. I think podcasts are a great way to learn about a topic very quickly as well as get excited about and motivated to make a change in your base habits.

This is the list of podcasts I am currently subscribed to in Overcast (my podcasting app of choice). I have loosely grouped them into three categories business, programming and general listening. They are listed in that order.

Business

Acquired (Ben Gilbert, David Rosenthal)

I only caught onto this recently as a co-worker mentioned the TikTok episode. Having said that, I have listened to 90% of the episodes in the archive already (I have spent a long time on planes and trains over the last month). Be warned, the episodes can get a little lengthy but it is all really insightful stuff so worth the time.

These guys do the background reading for you and give great overarching narratives sprinkled with a little analysis and investor insight. It is easy listening and if you want to learn a little more about the world of venture capital or hear stories of a large IPO, acquisitions or exits from the last few years, this is a great first stop.

Evolving for the Next Billion (GGV Capital)

Previously called 996 (after the Chinese work week), these guys are provide insights into the emerging super economies of our time - namely China, India and South East Asia as a region.

If you're looking to understand large business model trends coming out of China, this is one of the best quality sources for comentary and explaination that I have come across.

Founder Real Talk (Glenn Soloman of GGV Capital)

Another production from GGV Capital. This one seeks out the stories of founders and asks them hard questions. Glenn gets them to tell the stories of past battles won and lost.

Often when I finish listening to an episode I am inspired by the stories and decisions people have made. The episodes are usually less than and hour and make for good commuting entertainment.

Starting Greateness (Mike Maples Jr of Floodgate Capital)

A new podcast which only started in November 2019. Mike interviews titans of the tech world, people who have made it or are in the process, and breaks down what he considers to be the most important ideas in subsequent episodes.

Great for the stories. The lessons are not revolutionary but there is a lot of interesting history you can learn from the guests as they recount what they chose to do.

Software

To Be Continuous (Edith Harbaugh, Paul Biggar)

Episodes of this feel far and few between, not surprising since both hosts are busy running companies in California. I love the way these two discuss and explore ideas together, it is always conversational and light hearted. They are both increadiably smart cookies and I only wish they would publish more episodes.

Soft Skills Engineering (Dave Smith, Jamison Dance)

This show is another one of my favourites, I normally watch it each week that it comes out and when there is a repeat of an old episode I am disappointed.

The two hosts are sensative to tricky topics while keeping it extremely light hearted. They used to handout the advice - 'quit your job' a lot which is always a laugh, but they have toned that down recently.

They take two listener questions per episode, reading them aloud, discussing, making jokes and generally contributing some helpful thoughts on the topic.

Rustacean Station (Rust community)

This is a community project setup which allows anyone who wants to share rust audio with a large audiance to submit content and get it produced.

It contains interviews with shakers, movers and organisers of the rust community. A staple of the feed is the six weekly new language version release commentary discussing what all the new things mean for the rust eco-system and what is coming us in the next release.

General listening

Surprisingly Awesome (Adam Davidson, Adam McKay)

This show is no longer being produced unfortunately, they spun it out into another podcast with a slightly different angle. However, I enjoy the original episodes the most and have listened to them all. It is exactly what the title describes: they unpack topics and try to convince each other or guests that the topic being discuessed is indeed "Surprisingly Awesome".

You will learn a bunch of odd facts and appreciate how little you really know about the world around you.

Stuff you missed in history class (Holly, Tracy)

I only occasionally dip my toe in this one. I usually skim through the list of episodes for a couple seconds and find an historical event that peeks my fancy. I normally listen to this one on long car trips when I am tired or other people are interested in listening too.

Darknet Daries (Jack Rhysider)

Explains the dark side of the internet. Interviews with primary and secondary sources on some of the most high profile and secret missions every carried out online.

A must for people who want to understand the evolving threat landscape of the digital world and what is going on in international cyber warfare at the moment.

RNZ: Elemental (Allan Blackman, Alison Ballance)

This is a fun science podcast produced by the radio network of my home country (New Zealand!!). The two hosts worked through every element to celebrate 2019 being the 150th year of the periodic table.

They cover one or a group of related elements in each episode. They talk about the properties, modern uses and discovery. You are bound to learn something interesting in each episode and realise just how important chemistry is to our modern quality of life.

The Tim Ferris Show & The Joe Rogan Experience

These are both interview style podcast where the host asks interesting questions to interesting or semi-famous people. I rarely listen to these ones, I basically skim the episode feeds every couple weeks and see who has been interviewed. Only if I am really interested in the topic or have heard of the person then I download the episode and have a listen.
